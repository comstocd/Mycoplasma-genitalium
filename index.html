Recurrent Mycoplasma genitalium Urethritis with Sequential Treatment Failures

Diagnostic Gaps, Macrolide Resistance, and Fluoroquinolone–Mineral Chelation as Compounding Barriers to Cure

Comstock D, MD1  ·   Gareca MG, MD, FIDSA, FACP2
1 Department of Internal Medicine, Guthrie Lourdes Hospital, Binghamton, NY
2 Guthrie Corning · Guthrie Lourdes · Robert Packer Hospital · Guthrie Cortland Medical Center

A 34-year-old male presented with recurrent urethritis following urethral instrumentation, with documented M. genitalium NAAT positivity across five sequential antibiotic courses spanning October 2025 through February 2026. Presumed macrolide resistance, fluoroquinolone chelation by concurrent mineral supplementation (AUC reduction ~39%; Cmax ~59%), and the absence of resistance-guided therapy each contributed to treatment failure. Final microbiological cure was confirmed February 27, 2026. This case illustrates the convergence of diagnostic gaps, antimicrobial stewardship failures, and pharmacokinetic pitfalls that define the clinical challenge of M. genitalium in the United States.
